Maya Man (b. 1996) is a digital and generative artist whose practice spans browser interventions, algorithmic text-image systems, web projects, and hybrid installations. She interrogates notions of identity, performativity, and mediation in digital culture, especially focusing on how femininity, self-care, authenticity, and persona are constructed online. She frames the screen not merely as a passive surface but as an active site of performance and surveillance. Maya earned dual BAs in Computer Science and Media Studies from Pomona College, and in 2023 she completed her MFA in Media Art at UCLA.

Her CV lists solo shows and commissions including A Realistic Day In My Life Living in New York City (Whitney, 2024), FAKE IT TILL YOU MAKE IT: GIFT SHOP (Heavy Manners, L.A., 2024), FAKE IT TILL YOU MAKE IT (Art Blocks, 2022), Secrets From a Girl (SOOT, Tokyo, 2022), and I’m Feeling Lucky (Verse, London, 2023).

She has also participated in group exhibitions such as Artists Who Code (Vellum, Artsy) and Forest Temple I (Pretty Garden Club). bitforms gallery She is known for creating generative systems, browser-based works, and code-driven visual/textual hybrids. Maya On The Her homepage identifies her mission as exploring “identity culture on the Internet,” and she is represented digitally at mayaontheinter.net. Her projects often circulate in both web-native and institutional contexts, bridging net art, crypto/generative art, and digital art discourse. Through her work she addresses how screens mediate interior life, how personas are curated and consumed, and how our mediated environments may both reflect and distort notions of authenticity.
